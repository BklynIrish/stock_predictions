LSTM Model



The LSTM model is a sequential model. It learns a pattern by passing a sequence of models to different nodes and repeats the process a series of times. The underlying assumption of the LSTM model is that the stock will behave like it has in the past. This assumption can be dangerous as making variables can affect its performance First, it does not take into account the underlying health of the company. It disregards the fundementals which many investors rely on. Secondly, it does not account for news that may affect the company. For an example, CSCO recently announced better than expected earnings, but the LSTM model is not able to factor in the higher expectations, outside of a higher closing price for one particular day. In addition, major events around the country like the recent presidential election and second increase in COVID-19 cases do not have a direct affect on the model. While the LSTM misses critical aspects predicting the long term preformance in a stock, it is still as a proxy for short term movements based on historical data.
