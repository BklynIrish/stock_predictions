Limitations


The Long Short-Term (LSTM) model is a sequential model. It learns a pattern by passing a sequence of models to different nodes and repeats the process a series of times. The underlying assumption of the Long Short-Term model is that the stock will behave like it has in the past. This assumption can be dangerous as making variables can affect its performance. First, it does not take into account the underlying health of the company. It disregards the fundementals which many investors rely on. Secondly, it does not account for news that may affect the company. For an example, Cisco Systems recently announced better than expected earnings, but the LSTM model is not able to factor in the higher expectations, outside of a higher closing price for one particular day. In addition, major events around the country like the recent presidential election and second increase in COVID-19 cases will not immediately have a direct affect on the model.




Possible Improvements


The Long-Short Term Model is a starting point in building more advanced models. Given the time constraint for the porject we were only able to build a single-variate model. Building a multivariate model would allow us to factor in more of the drivers of the stock prices. We could use a multivariate LSTM model. This model would be similar to the one we already have, except it is looking at more factors when making its prediction. We could also ultilze a combination of mulitple LSTM models and a multivariate linear regression to more accurately predict future stock prices. We could forecast the movement of different fundementals using LSTM models and then use a multivariate linear regression to predict stock prices with the results from the LSTM models.


Conclusion


The Long-Short Term Model is not a perfect tool in forecasting stocks because it isn't able to fully factor in some of the drivers behind a stock's price, like fundemental analysis, growth catalysts, and unexpected news headlines. However, it does provide value in helping predict stock prices based on past trends.
